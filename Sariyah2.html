The origin of the name "Sariaya" is surrounded by various legends, folktales, and stories passed down through generations. One account links the name to an old river called “Sadyaya,” which gradually evolved into “Sariaya” over time, especially after the arrival of the Spaniards—a shift considered natural by the people of Batangas. Another tale suggests that the name came from a local woman named Saria. According to the story, a lost Spanish soldier asked her for the name of the town, but she misunderstood and gave her own name instead, leading to the town being called “Sariaya.” A third version tells of Moro pirates who frequently raided the area. During one such attack, they looted the public office and chapel, searching for riches. Instead of treasures, they discovered documents outlining the town’s local laws, which they found to be exceptionally fair. Impressed, the pirates remarked that although the town was poor, it had just laws, and they referred to it as “Shariah,” a Muslim term meaning “good law,” which some believe became the town’s name.
